Overview: The Boeing 747 has been one of the most iconic planes ever. The “Queen of the Skies” was the biggest commercial aircraft until 2006 with the release of the A380, and is easily one of the most recognizable aircrafts with its hump. The 747 is also Boeings only plane with four engines, two on each wing. British Airways, Korean Air, Lufthansa, and China Airlines are the main buyers of this expensive jet with the new 8i model costing $351.4 million USD. When first released in 1968, people were tentative about the 747, and the only airlines that bought more than one at first was Pan Am. The 747 is also well known for being the aircraft of the call signal Air Force One. Nasa also used the 747 to transfer its Endeavor space shuttle to LA. The 747 has also been in some notable crashes, such as the disastrous Tenerife Airport disaster, where a KLM 747 rammed into a Pan Am 747 on the runway due to low visibility, killing 583 people, with only 61 surviving. Many people are saying that the 747 will die out in the next couple decades, due to the fuel efficiency of the new 777, 787, and A350. If that is true or not, the Boeing 747 will certainly be remembered as one of the most beautifully designed planes ever.
