Overview: The Boeing 757 was a mid-size narrow body twin jet airliner produced by Boeing between 1981 and 2004. The 757 was produced in two vastly different fuselage lengths. The original 757-100 and 200 average for a fuselage length, but the new 757-300 was unusually long, and still has the longest fuselage today. The 757 had Pratt & Whitney PW2000 engines, and some had Rolls-Royce RB211 engines (like Donald Trump’s). Overall, the 757 wasn’t a huge success in the market, for it was supposed to compete with the booming A320 family, and it was unable to do so.
